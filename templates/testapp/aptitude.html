Aptitude Exam Section

Exam Overview

The Aptitude exam is designed to assess your logical reasoning, quantitative aptitude, verbal ability, and problem-solving skills. This exam evaluates your analytical thinking and decision-making capabilities essential for technical and professional roles.

Exam Structure

  • Duration: 60 minutes
  • Total Questions: 60
  • Question Types: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Passing Score: 60%
  • Negative Marking: 0.25 marks deducted for each wrong answer

Sections and Topics Covered

1. Quantitative Aptitude (25 Questions)

  1. Number System - HCF, LCM, Divisibility rules, Prime numbers
  2. Percentages - Basic calculations, Profit and Loss, Discount
  3. Ratio and Proportion - Simple and compound ratios, Partnership
  4. Time and Work - Work efficiency, Pipes and cisterns
  5. Time, Speed and Distance - Average speed, Relative speed, Trains
  6. Simple and Compound Interest - Interest calculations, Growth problems
  7. Averages - Mean, Weighted average
  8. Algebra - Linear equations, Quadratic equations, Inequalities
  9. Geometry - Triangles, Circles, Rectangles, Area and Perimeter
  10. Mensuration - Volume, Surface area of 3D shapes
  11. Data Interpretation - Tables, Bar graphs, Pie charts, Line graphs

2. Logical Reasoning (20 Questions)

  1. Series - Number series, Letter series, Mixed series
  2. Coding-Decoding - Letter coding, Number coding, Substitution
  3. Blood Relations - Family tree problems
  4. Direction Sense - Finding directions and distances
  5. Seating Arrangement - Linear, Circular, Rectangular arrangements
  6. Puzzles - Logic puzzles, Scheduling problems
  7. Syllogism - Logical conclusions from statements
  8. Venn Diagrams - Set theory problems
  9. Clocks and Calendars - Time calculations, Day problems
  10. Statements and Assumptions - Logical deductions

3. Verbal Ability (15 Questions)

  1. Synonyms and Antonyms - Word meanings and opposites
  2. Sentence Correction - Grammar and syntax errors
  3. Reading Comprehension - Understanding passages
  4. Fill in the Blanks - Context-based word selection
  5. Spotting Errors - Grammatical mistakes identification
  6. Idioms and Phrases - Common expressions and their meanings
  7. One Word Substitution - Single word for phrases
  8. Para Jumbles - Sentence rearrangement

Question Distribution

  • Quantitative Aptitude: 25 questions (1.5 marks each)
  • Logical Reasoning: 20 questions (1.5 marks each)
  • Verbal Ability: 15 questions (1 mark each)
  • Total Marks: 82.5

Aptitude Exam Specific Rules

  1. Calculators are NOT allowed - all calculations must be done mentally or on scratch paper
  2. Each question has four options with only one correct answer
  3. Negative marking applies: -0.25 marks for wrong answers
  4. Unattempted questions carry zero marks (no negative marking)
  5. Time management is crucial - avoid spending too much time on one question
  6. You can skip questions and return to them later if time permits
  7. Read questions carefully before selecting answers
  8. For data interpretation questions, analyze the data before looking at options

Sample Questions

Quantitative Aptitude:

Q1. If the cost price of 12 pens is equal to the selling price of 10 pens, what is the profit percentage?

a) 10%
b) 15%
c) 20%
d) 25%

Q2. A train 150 meters long crosses a pole in 15 seconds. What is the speed of the train in km/hr?

a) 36 km/hr
b) 40 km/hr
c) 45 km/hr
d) 50 km/hr

Logical Reasoning:

Q1. Complete the series: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ?

a) 40
b) 42
c) 44
d) 46

a) APDCPZPM

b) Precise

d) Generous

a) Neither of
b) have completed

Time Management Strategy

  • First 5 minutes: Quickly scan all questions to identify easy ones
  • Minutes 6-25: Attempt easy and moderate questions from all sections
  • Minutes 26-50: Focus on difficult questions and data interpretation
  • Last 10 minutes: Review answers and attempt skipped questions if confident
  • Allocate approximately: Quant (30 min), Reasoning (20 min), Verbal (10 min)

Preparation Tips

  • Practice mental math to improve calculation speed
  • Learn shortcuts and formulas for common problem types
  • Practice previous year question papers and mock tests
  • Build vocabulary by reading newspapers and books regularly
  • Work on improving accuracy before focusing on speed
  • Learn to eliminate obviously wrong options quickly
  • Practice time-bound tests to improve speed
  • Review mistakes and understand the correct approach
  • Don't leave easy questions unattempted due to time pressure
  • Stay calm and don't panic if you encounter difficult questions

Important Formulas to Remember

  • Profit/Loss % = (Profit or Loss / CP) × 100
  • Speed = Distance / Time
  • Simple Interest = (P × R × T) / 100
  • Compound Interest = P(1 + R/100)^T - P
  • Average = Sum of observations / Number of observations
  • Work = Time × Efficiency
  • Area of Circle = πr²
  • Perimeter of Rectangle = 2(Length + Breadth)
  • Pythagorean Theorem = a² + b² = c²

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not reading questions carefully and missing key details
  • Making calculation errors due to hurry
  • Attempting too many difficult questions and running out of time
  • Random guessing when unsure (remember negative marking)
  • Not managing time effectively across sections
  • Spending too much time on data interpretation questions
  • Ignoring units in calculations (km vs m, hours vs minutes)
  • Not double-checking answers for silly mistakes

  • Avoid learning new topics
  • Stay positive and confident
